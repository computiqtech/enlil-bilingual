Comprehensive Analysis

⚡ Key Findings

  • RMSN achieved correlation coefficients up to 1.0 for SPI-6, SPI-9, and SPI-12 — outstanding performance.
  • For SPI-3, RMSN achieved R=0.7881 with RMSE=0.6669, reflecting good short-term variability capture.
  • DMSN was most effective for SPI-12 (R=0.7684), indicating suitability for long-term patterns.

⚠️ Drought Risk Assessment

  • Forecasts indicate continued moderate-to-severe drought patterns during summer months (June–September).
  • The 2024–2027 period may experience sharper SPI-3 variability, requiring proactive planning.
  • Winter months (December–February) show positive SPI values in most forecasts.

🛡️ Recommendations

  • Use RMSN as the primary forecast model, with DMSN as a reference for long-term indices.
  • Develop contingency plans for anticipated severe drought periods during summer months.
  • Integrate these forecasts with existing early warning systems at the Iraqi Meteorological Authority.
1

What is SPI?

Standardized Precipitation Index. Negative = drought, positive = wet, near-zero = normal.

2

Reading the Colors

Green = normal, orange = moderate drought, red = severe drought, blue = wet conditions.

3

RMSN vs DMSN

RMSN: more accurate overall — use as primary. DMSN: better for SPI-12 long-term patterns.

💾

Data Source & Methodology

📍 Data Source

  • ● Baghdad Meteorological Station
  • ● Monthly rainfall data
  • ● 1985–2021 historical | 2022–2030 forecast

🧠 RMSN

Stacked Recurrent Neural Network. Uses multiple recurrent layers to capture temporal patterns in rainfall data.

🧱 DMSN

Stacked Dilated Neural Network. Uses dilated convolutions to capture long-term climate patterns effectively.
